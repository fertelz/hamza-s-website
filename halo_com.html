HALO combat evolved




Released in 2001 as a launch title for the original Xbox, Halo: Combat Evolved revolutionized first-person shooters and became the foundation of one of gaming's most iconic franchises.
Developed by Bungie, the game introduced players to Master Chief, a super-soldier known as a Spartan, who battles the alien Covenant on a mysterious ring-shaped world called Halo.

With its gripping campaign, open-ended level design, and tight gunplay, Halo redefined console shooters.
The introduction of regenerating shields, vehicular combat, and a rich sci-fi narrative set it apart from other FPS games of the time.
Halo's local multiplayer also became a fan favorite, leading to legendary LAN parties before online gaming took off.

Beyond its single-player experience, Halo: Combat Evolved laid the groundwork for the success of Halo 2 and the rise of Xbox as a dominant gaming platform.
Its influence can still be felt in modern shooters, and its legacy remains strong through Halo: The Master Chief Collection.
