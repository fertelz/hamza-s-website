POKEMON




Pokemon debuted in 1996 with Pokemon Red and Green (later released as Red and Blue internationally) for the Game Boy.
Created by Satoshi Tajiri and developed by Game Freak,
the game introduced players to a world where they could catch, train, and battle creatures known as Pokemon.
The goal was to become the Pokemon Champion while completing the Pokedex.

The game's success led to a multimedia empire, including trading cards, an anime series, and countless sequels.
Pokemon Gold and Silver expanded the formula with new Pokemon and a real-time clock,
while Pokemon X and Y brought the series into 3D.
The franchise remains one of the most successful in gaming history, continuing to evolve with titles like Pokemon Sword and Shield and Poke mon Legends: Arceus.
